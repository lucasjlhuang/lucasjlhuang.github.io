Introduction

VoGro is a volunteer management platform, initially designed to improve the lives of those impacted by the pandemic. With more time and development we evolved, encompassing community and social opportunities as well. We wanted to make this platform as accessible as possible. Following the development of our dashboard, we set out to enable a helping hand.

Problem statement

VoGro was created to help the community and aid those limited by the pandemic and its restrictions. But VoGro was struggling to connect volunteers to the community. There was limited outreach and ways to recruit. How might we bring opportunities to volunteers in the midst of a pandemic, and bring help to those in need?

Researching a solution

Step-one was looking into what was out there, the different volunteering platforms on the market. We were looking into what worked, and what we could improve on.

Step-two was finding out what our users were saying.

We prioritized researching 4 different volunteering companies that operated within North America to identify key features in a successful product, that ours was missing. We had to make sure that our process was straight to the point, and any barriers were reduced or taken out all together.

Our main goal was to figure out what we needed to create a MVP for our current users. We already had over 400+ volunteers in our system and needed a solution fast. With our findings we went straight to the drawing board and created our first iteration.

Communicating through E-mails in our old system was just not a viable option for the scale we were at. We opted for a few key features that allows the users to stay within the app - a one stop shop for volunteers to help right away.

üí¨ An in-app social hub for volunteers to message team-leads, or event organizes.

üó∫Ô∏è An informative mapping system that displays surrounding tasks.

üóÇÔ∏è An organized task-list that helps volunteer stay updated.

Once our MVP was complete we launched right away. We needed a product in the market to gain new users, and increase the tasks completion rate or we'd lose trust with the community we were meant to help.

While we had an overwhelming amount of ideas for our app, like blog posts, a social media aspect, a community market, we chose to focus on what would help us the most immediately. We launched with three main focuses for our app.

Organized Task List

An important feature we wanted to implement is our task tracker, which provided users the ability to stay up-to-date and informed with their commitments.

Easy to Understand Map

We built a familiar map feature that gives our users a visual representation of their location. Understanding how far the task is, or how much time it might take.

Detailed Descriptions

It is important our users know how and what they are going to be doing. We wanted full transparency between event hosts and the volunteers.

And we saw success. Our volunteer to task connection rates increased by 15%, and our task completion rates increased by 20%.

But we knew we could do better. Now that the bandaid was on, we wanted to continue improving.

User testing

We tested our first iteration with a group of 15 members, all of who were either active or formerly active volunteers with us.

The primary goal was to enable the success of members in finding and accepting tasks. The group was given a prototype via Figma and monitored/recorded over a video call as they completed tasks given to them. The metrics of this study were:

Overall users found the designs easy to follow and were able to complete all tasks given to them. The problem areas of the designs were found in the in-between of the process. Our group reported only a 73% satisfaction rate going through the processes of the given tasks, despite the 100% completion rate. This left us with a 27% room for improvement.

The new and improved

Using our user-testing data, we created a new modern design for the app. Further rounds of user testing showed us we moved in the right direction seeing a level of 90% in user satisfaction and a 45% increase in task completion by our volunteers. Now we were ready to lend a helping hand.
