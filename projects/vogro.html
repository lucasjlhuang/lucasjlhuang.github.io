SUMMARY

CONTEXT

VoGro's connection to users was through email, with no digital platforms. They experienced high rates of user decline, task incompletion, and general negative feedback. My role was to spearhead a mobile application to get them back on track, handling the end-to-end design process.

STAKEHOLDERS

Engineering team
Founders
Event managers

MY ROLE

Mobile Product Lead

LENGTH

24 weeks

STEP 1: FIND EXISTING SOLUTIONS

Industry standards and current solutions

Before starting the project I led research on what products already existed in this space. We needed to ideate and get a MVP to market to stop the drop-off of users as soon as possible.

STEP 2: USER RESEARCH

Finding out what our users were saying

I had to understand what the current users needs and wants were, and what they didn't think was working. I used this to develop a clear design strategy, validate future decisions, and define a baseline for success.

IDEATION AND BRAINSTORM

Approach the problem one-by-one

To efficiently address each pain-point I collected from our users, I listed each identified problem and designed a solution for each problem individually. Doing this I provide thorough solutions, and prioritized issues based on levels of importance, ensuring I meet the needs of the users.

Iteration finished, MVP complete

Once our MVP was complete we launched right away. We needed a product in the market to retain and increase users, and increase the amount of tasks being completed or we'd lose trust with the community we were meant to help.

While myself and the team had an overwhelming amount of ideas for the app. I chose to focus on what would help us the most immediately, and stuck to core features I identified during our research phase.

Short-term Tracked Results

And we saw success

Short-term analytics showed us that volunteer to task connection rates increased by 35%, and our task completion rates increased by 40%. User-retention soared to 77%. But we knew we could do better. Now that the bandaid was on, we wanted to continue improving.

Validating our designs

Rapid user-testing in our AGILE environment

Keeping up our design momentum, I immediately switched gears into rapid-fire user-testing. With a group of 20 users I began looking for pain-points or features I missed in the initial development of the MVP.

Through observational testing over video interviews and prepared surveys, I found that while the product worked, it was tedious and slowing down our users. Leading to dissatisfaction and an inefficient flow.

Our Final Solution

Re-thinking, re-designing

Using the previous round of user-testing, and keeping in mind our findings from the initial user research, I created a final set of designs. This included a re-thought out approach to giving our volunteers freedom and ease of use and an updated UI to officialize the app. Further analytic tracking showed us that we moved in the right direction, with our task-connection rates increasing an additional 15%, and task completion rates increasing another 30%.

Finishing VoGro I laid out a design-roadmap for their future. Outlining design considerations such as a calendar feature, a QR ID code for signing into events, and a user friendly onboarding process.
