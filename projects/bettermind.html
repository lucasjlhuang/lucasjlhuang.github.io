The Problem

University students take on many responsibilities, but also stress. This leads to many reporting mental health issues. Approximately 75% of university students struggle with these issues. More than half of those report feeling depressed or anxious. At this stage in their life they may not have the tools to address these issues themselves. Outside resources are expensive or inaccessible.

The solution?

Enter Bettermind, an online service that connects those struggling with their mental health with others equipped to give them the tools to help themselves, or just lend an ear. Accessible through any device and requiring no payment or subscription, ensuring someone is always there for you.

User testing

Users

Of our 48 interviewees, 23 agreed to use our prototype and give feedback over the course of a month.

Employees

We hired 10 counseling, psychology, or social work graduates to work as our therapists. They had varying schedules to ensure we had 24/7 coverage for our users.

Our users had full access to Bettermind's services, being able to call, text, or video chat 24/7 with our therapists. Over the duration of testing we would collect analytics such as user drop-off points, usage and feature popularity, and other data from surveys.

Adjusting direction

Two weeks in we reviewed our data. We found that most cancellations were being made in the leading days to the appointment. With almost 65% of bookings being cancelled early. Users stated that their plans kept changing, overlapping the booking. Most of those who did attend their bookings stated they felt obligated or forgot but saw the reminder.

A second point of interest was, 56% of users stated they wanted the ability to choose whether the person on call with them had their mic/camera enabled, or if they responded. Some users just wanted to vent and be heard, not have a conversation.

Change one
Remove the booking feature and full steer into on-demand 24/7 service.

Change two
Give full camera and mic control to the caller.

A/B TESTING

To find out if our changes made a positive or negative impact we went with my favorite, A/B testing. Randomly assigning some users version A (Old) and some users version B (New).

Results
