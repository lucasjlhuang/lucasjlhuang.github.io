IDENTIFYING EXISTING FRICTION POINTS

Understanding the Gap and Opportunities

To Identify areas for improvement, develop a clear design strategy and gave me a basis to validate design decisions I studied our funnel and current UI. This enabled measuring the project success and make adjustments, using the existing funnel as a benchmark.

FINDING THE WHY

Users are falling off. Why?

Half of the users making it through our project entry point drop-off between categorization and preference selection. It narrows our funnel significantly and gives me a direction to what needs to be changed. To fund my decisions with data, I conducted interviews.

Analyzing the data

Using existing research, our target demographic, and interview findings, I compiled data to find our best next steps. Our users want a quick and seamless process, they want another person to hear them not necessarily talk with them, and they don't want to share their personal information.

IDEATION

Redefining the product

So how do I design around that? First, reduce friction by limiting clicks to get to end-result. Second cut out unnecessary information collection. Third push preference selection to point-of-entry to accelerate the process.

Let them vote on the solution

After designing solutions for each problem, I presented them to the team for voting to ensure collective decision making and to consider diverse perspectives. This approach promotes buy-in and ownership among team members leading to better implementation and execution of the chosen solution.

BUILDING THE SOLUTION

New features, new flow, better conversion

With team and research data validating design direction, I went to the drawing board to create our new solution. After rounds of A/B testing, I was happy with our results, and voila. We saw a 26% increase in user-follow-through, and as a bonus re-booking rates, proving our solution was effective and users enjoyed it.
