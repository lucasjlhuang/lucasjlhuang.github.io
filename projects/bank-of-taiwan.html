CONTEXT

Bank of Taiwan, is in a competitive race to acquire the rapidly growing English market. The country has seen mass English speakers immigrating, but many companies have not designed around this. My role was to help lead the English facing app to increase their market share with target demographics.

CURRENT PRODUCT

Their current design

Bank of Taiwan's current product didn't include some key features to acquire English clients. For starters, the did not have English translations; their users screenshotted each screen and translated them until they simply remembered what to click.

They also included a lot of features that did not make sense for an English client to use. For example, banking features that are only legally permitted for Taiwanese citizens to use. This only created confusion, frustration, and many errors for their users. Being one of the most prominent bank's in the country, and already having a massive English user base, this had to change immediately.

COMPETITORS DESIGNS

Their competitors were adapting

I noticed that their main competitors, the other largest market controlling banks in the country were updating their apps, sites, and services to accommodate the emerging market, and modernizing their designs.

My solution? Make the users feel at home

Following western banking designs would encourage users to use a familiar product. They would intuitively understand navigating the app, using the features, and completing their banking. Taiwanese apps are set-up in an entirely different way than their western counterparts. Making a separate English facing product would help accommodate both demographics.
