Designer by accident

Believe it or not, I ended up as a designer completely by accident. Every week leading up to my highschool graduation I had a new career plan - professional gamer, architect, lawyer, industrial designer, doctor, graphic designer. I had no idea what I wanted to do with my life, all I knew was I liked creating. I was making functional model cars, popsicle bridges holding up to 90lbs, bus stations with working electronics, and I lived in autocad, but never once touched digital products. I only applied to Global Business and Digital Arts with Waterloo because my sister was already in the program (shout out Sarah). Like Bob Ross says, it was a happy accident. Having the opportunity to be creative but still use logic and research to solve my problems really scratched the itch. From there I found opportunities to grow and challenge myself as a designer and i'm so happy I stumbled in on it.

Learning through exploration

After graduating I decided to travel the world and find ways to learn about design through experiences and journeys. I studied design in different cultures, built products overseas, and had a ton of adventures. I've learned a lot about communicating through design, because often times I didn't understand the language of where I was. Design was a way for me to reach others.

Everything said, I hope you love my portfolio because I loved making it.
